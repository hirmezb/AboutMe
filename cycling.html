Why I Enjoy Cycling So Much?

I started cycling daily while I was an undergraduate student at UC Davis, one of the most bike friendly campuses. UCD has the 10th largest campus in the US so riding a bike was almost a necessity to make it to classes on time. Over time, cycling switched from being a necessary chore to an amazing hobby. I would go on late night rides on campus while listening to music or just to try to relax, especially during finals week. Being at Davis (the city), I was forced to become a better cyclist having to deal with so many cars on the road; this made me a more confident biker and in turn, made me fall in love with cycling even more. In my fourth year at Davis, I made a new friend from my anatomy course, and he happened to be on the cycling team. We started going out biking together, only short distances at first (5-10 miles) and then slowly increased it to where we would cycle for 50-60 miles a ride without getting too tired. This gave me the courage to want to try completing a century ride (100 mile ride nonstop). I followed an 8 week plan to make sure I was in good enough shape to be able to do it without injuring myself. After the 8 weeks were up, my friend and I cycled the 100 mile trip together, riding up from Davis to Lake Berryessa, Napa vineyards, and lakes a long the way. This was definitely the most challenging physical act I've done at that point.

After moving back home after school ended, I treated myself to a Specialized Rouabix bike, which was far more comfortable than the one I used for the century ride, a 2018 Specialized Tarmac. This made it easier for me to continue my long distance rides at home. One of the things I love most about riding is that I can go explore new roads and see new things while I keep my camera in my backpack. This helps me combine both passions into one, and there is nothing more fun than getting to take photos of a new scene after a long bike ride.
