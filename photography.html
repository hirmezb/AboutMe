Why Photography?

I grew up with a dad and a grandpa that loved photography. I just picked up my dad's camera and started snapping pictures probably when I was 4 or 5 years old; Of course, they were horrible (as expected haha), but they still remain today. I think that's why I enjoy photography so much: it captures a fond memory and makes it last a lifetime and some more. I never get tired of seeing old family photos, both from when I was a kid to well before I was born. It feels weird that you're able to look into the lives of people who might've passed a long time ago and still picture them clearly.

After growing up and getting more experience, I went from using a simple Fujifilm snap-and-shoot to a Sony A7RIII DSLR. I mostly do landscape photography now, but I also like portrait and urban photography. I began to edit my own photos using Adobe Lightroom and Photoshop. Recently, I bought a printer to print out our photos at home (my dad, brother, and I). This gives it even more meaning now: the feeling of physically holding something in your hands that you created is just amazing!! I'll show you a list of the equipment I use from beginning to end, but you really don't need all that; just some passion and the will to go out and take photos.
